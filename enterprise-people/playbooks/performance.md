# Performance Playbook — review cycle, calibration, PIP, termination

Reference for `/people --perform`. Two iron laws govern everything here:
1. **No surprises.** A PIP or termination that surprises the employee means the feedback
   system failed first. Fix that failure before acting on the person.
2. **The documentation sufficiency test**: before any adverse action, ask "Would this file
   persuade a neutral third party?" Contemporaneous, specific, dated, and the feedback
   demonstrably DELIVERED (a note in a drawer is not delivered feedback).

## 1. Review cycle design (startup standard)

Semi-annual lightweight cycle + continuous feedback between cycles. Annual-only is too slow
for startup role change; quarterly full reviews burn manager time. Components in order:

```
Self-review → Manager review → (360/peer at senior levels) → Calibration → Comp linkage → Delivery conversations
```

- **Self-review**: goals recap + evidence, 30–45 min budget, same questions as the manager form.
- **Manager review**: written against LEVEL EXPECTATIONS (from the comp architecture), with
  full-period examples required — recency bias is designed out by requiring at least one
  example from each half of the period.
- **360/peer input**: senior levels and managers only at startup scale; 3–4 reviewers,
  structured prompts, manager synthesizes (raw comments breed score-settling).
- **Rating scale**: 3–5 points with behavioral anchors. Recommended 4-point:
  | Rating | Anchor |
  |--------|--------|
  | Exceeds level | Consistently operates above level expectations; promotion conversation warranted |
  | Meets level | Fully performing at level — the healthy default, most of the company |
  | Approaching | Meets some expectations; specific gaps named with development actions |
  | Below | Sustained gap against level expectations; triggers the pre-PIP gate |
  No forced ranking at startup scale — stack-ranking 15 people manufactures a "bottom 10%."
- **Comp linkage**: merit matrix — increase % = f(rating, position-in-band). Higher rating +
  lower in band → largest increase; top-of-band performers get the promotion conversation, not
  a band-breaking raise.

### Calibration (the step that makes ratings mean anything)

Managers meet before ratings are final. Rules:
1. Review every proposed "Exceeds" and "Below" with evidence; spot-check "Meets."
2. Standard-setting question: "Would this person get the same rating on your team?"
3. Distribution GUIDANCE not quotas (if 90% are "Exceeds," the bar is wrong; no one is forced
   down to hit a curve).
4. Check the rating distribution by demographic before finalizing — unexplained skew is a
   BLOCKING finding.
5. Calibration notes are retained: they are the consistency evidence if a rating is ever challenged.

### Review template (per person)

```markdown
# Performance Review — <Name>, <Level> — <Period>
1. Goals recap vs. results — each goal, outcome, evidence (links, metrics)
2. Competency assessment vs. <level> expectations — per competency: rating + example
3. Strengths (2–3) — specific, evidenced
4. Growth areas (1–2) — each with a concrete development action and support named
5. Overall rating — <anchor language, verbatim from the scale>
6. Career conversation notes — trajectory, next-level gaps if promotion-track
7. Calibration flag — [ ] reviewed in calibration <date>
```

## 2. The pre-PIP gate (run BEFORE drafting any PIP)

Answer all four in writing. Any "no" → STOP; fix that first.

1. **Has specific feedback been delivered?** Documented conversations with dates, the gap
   named concretely, expectations stated. "I hinted at it in 1:1s" is a no.
2. **Is the gap specific and measurable?** "Bad attitude" fails. "Missed 3 of 4 sprint
   commitments in Q2 (list); ship dates communicated late twice (dates)" passes.
3. **Is this a role-fit or manager problem?** Ask first: Did the role change under them? Is
   the manager giving contradictory direction? Has anyone succeeded in this role? A PIP cannot
   fix a manager failure — it just documents the wrong person.
4. **Any risk indicators?** Recent protected activity (harassment/discrimination complaint,
   FMLA/other protected leave, accommodation request, workers-comp claim, whistleblowing),
   protected-class considerations incl. age 40+, recent return from leave. **Any yes →
   employment counsel BEFORE the PIP is drafted, not after.** Adverse action shortly after
   protected activity creates the retaliation claim by timing alone — sequence and document.

Then decide and state plainly which this is: **a genuine turnaround tool** (you would be
pleased if they recovered — real support, real belief) or **documented progression to exit**
(the decision is effectively made; the PIP creates fairness and a record). Both are
legitimate. Not knowing which is not — it produces mixed signals and half-hearted support that
fails in both directions.

## 3. PIP template

```markdown
# Performance Improvement Plan — <Name>, <Role>
Date: <date> · Duration: <30/60/90 days> · Manager: <name> · HR reviewer: <name>

## 1. Performance gaps
Per gap: specific incidents WITH DATES, the expectation not met, prior feedback
given (date + channel). No characterizations — observable facts only.

## 2. Expectations (measurable, mapped to role outcomes)
Per expectation: the measurable bar, how it will be measured, by when.
Achievable within the PIP window by a competent person in the role — an
impossible PIP is evidence of pretext.

## 3. Support provided
Concrete: training, pairing, re-prioritized workload, manager availability.
"We are here to help" is not support.

## 4. Check-ins
Weekly, calendared now. Each check-in documented: progress against each
expectation, on/off track stated explicitly in writing.

## 5. Consequences
"Failure to meet and sustain these expectations may result in further action,
up to and including termination of employment." Present tense, plain, once.

## 6. At-will preservation
"This plan does not alter the at-will nature of your employment; either party
may end the employment relationship at any time."

## 7. Acknowledgment
Employee signature = receipt, not agreement ("acknowledgment of receipt only").
Refusal to sign: note "declined to sign, copy provided" with a witness — never
escalate over the signature itself.

HR sign-off: __________ (required before delivery)
```

Duration guidance: 30 days for execution gaps with fast feedback loops; 60 standard; 90 for
long-cycle roles. Extending a failing PIP repeatedly signals the turnaround decision was never
real — decide at the end date.

## 4. Termination checklist

### Legal-review triggers — ANY of these → employment attorney before proceeding (hard gate)

- Protected-class member with any recent complaint, leave, or accommodation (retaliation timing)
- Age 40+ (ADEA), pregnancy, disability, or recent protected activity of any kind
- **Severance in exchange for a release — ALWAYS attorney-drafted.** If 40+: OWBPA — 21-day
  consideration + 7-day revocation; group terminations: 45-day + a disclosure schedule of
  decisional-unit ages/titles
- Any litigation threat, demand letter, or agency charge (EEOC/state) on file
- Executive, equity-heavy, or contract-modified employment
- **RIF/layoff**: selection criteria documented BEFORE selecting names; adverse-impact
  analysis on the resulting list; WARN check (100+ employees federal; mini-WARN states lower —
  CA at 75, NY, NJ); consistent severance framework across the group

### Execution checklist (single termination, gate cleared)

- [ ] Documentation file passes the sufficiency test (neutral-third-party standard)
- [ ] Consistency check: has anyone else done the same thing and been treated differently?
      (Inconsistency is the discrimination fact pattern — resolve before acting)
- [ ] Final pay computed per WORK STATE law — CA: same day, in hand, for involuntary
      (including accrued vacation payout); other states vary same-day → next payroll. Verify
      the specific state's rule at run time.
- [ ] COBRA (or state mini-COBRA) notice queued via benefits admin
- [ ] Equity: exercise-window notice in writing (typically 90 days post-termination; ISO→NSO
      conversion after 90 days disclosed); never advise on their exercise decision
- [ ] Severance decision made (if any → attorney-drafted release, always)
- [ ] Access revocation staged for the conversation hour — not the day before (signals), not
      days after (risk)
- [ ] Logistics: who speaks (manager + HR witness), script drafted (short, decided, humane —
      the decision is final; do not debate it in the room), equipment return, references stance
- [ ] Separation comms plan: team message (no performance details — "X's last day was Y; Z
      picks up A"), customer handoffs if client-facing
- [ ] The conversation itself is HUMAN-delivered. Always. The skill drafts the script; a
      person says it.

## 5. Manager coaching guide (ship with every cycle)

- Feedback formula: situation → observed behavior → impact → expectation. Within days, not
  saved for the review.
- The 1:1 is the employee's meeting; performance concerns still get named there explicitly —
  "this is a performance concern" said out loud, then documented same-day in a dated note.
  That sentence is what makes later action fair and defensible.
- Delivery scripts per rating, including the hard one: "Your rating is Approaching. Here is
  the specific gap, here is the plan, here is what changes if it doesn't close."
- Documentation hygiene: contemporaneous (written when it happened), specific (facts, dates,
  quotes), delivered (the employee heard it), even-handed (praise documented too — a file
  with only negatives after years of silence reads as pretext).
